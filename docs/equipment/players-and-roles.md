# Players & Roles

Thousand is most commonly played by **three players**, each playing for
themselves. Within a single deal, players take on temporary **roles** that
change every hand.

## The three permanent positions

```
       Forehand
          ▲
          │
  Rearhand ◄─► Middlehand
```

- **Forehand** — the player to the dealer's left; leads the first trick
  before bidding and acts first in the auction.
- **Middlehand** — the player to the forehand's left.
- **Rearhand (the Dealer)** — shuffles, deals, and acts last.

The dealer rotates **clockwise** after each deal.

## Roles within a single deal

Roles are assigned at the end of the auction phase and last only for that
deal:

| Role | How they get it | What they do |
|---|---|---|
| **Declarer** *(also "soloist", "winner of the auction")* | Wins the bidding | Takes the talon, may raise their bid, names trump, and plays alone against both opponents. |
| **Defenders** | The other two players | Cooperate **without sharing information** to stop the declarer from making their contract. |
| **Dealer** | Rotates clockwise | Shuffles and deals; opponent of the dealer to their left is forehand. |

:::warning[Defenders cannot collude openly]
Defenders may not show cards, signal, or discuss strategy. They simply share
the goal of preventing the declarer from making their contract — and each
defender keeps their **own** independent score.
:::

## Player counts other than three

Thousand has well-developed variants for **2, 4 and even 6 players**. They
adjust roles as follows:

- **2 players** — head-to-head; usually no talon, or a face-up talon. See
  [Two-player Variant](../variations/two-player).
- **4 players** — usually played in **fixed partnerships** (across the
  table); the dealer sits out each hand or plays as a fourth defender. See
  [Four-player Variant](../variations/four-player).
- **6 players** — two teams of three with cross-table partnerships; rare but
  documented in some Russian sources.

The role names **declarer / defenders / dealer** carry over across variants;
only the number of seats and the partnership structure change.
