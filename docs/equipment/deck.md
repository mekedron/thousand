# The Deck

Thousand uses a **24-card stripped deck** — a standard 52-card pack with all
cards from **2 through 8 removed**. Only the high cards remain.

## Cards in the deck

| Suit | Cards |
|---|---|
| ♠ Spades | 9, 10, J, Q, K, A |
| ♣ Clubs | 9, 10, J, Q, K, A |
| ♦ Diamonds | 9, 10, J, Q, K, A |
| ♥ Hearts | 9, 10, J, Q, K, A |

That is **6 cards × 4 suits = 24 cards** in total.

## Why 24 cards?

The stripped deck is shared with several relatives in the Marriage family
(Schnapsen, Sixty-Six, Bezique). It keeps every hand high-value and ensures
that every card matters: in a 24-card game every card has either point value
in tricks, marriage potential, or both.

:::tip[Where to find a deck]
Any standard playing-card deck works — simply remove cards 2 through 8 before
play. Dedicated **Преферанс** (Preference) or **Schnapsen** decks ship as
24-card packs ready to use.
:::

## Optional accessories

- **A score pad** — Thousand scores are tracked as running totals to 1000,
  which is hard to do mentally. Printed score sheets with three columns are
  the most common form.
- **A pencil** — pen-and-paper scoring is universal; some tables use bidding
  chips to mark current bids during the auction.

See [Card Ranking](./card-ranking) for the rank and point value of each
individual card.
