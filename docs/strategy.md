# Strategy

Thousand rewards **disciplined bidding** and **careful card management** far
more than spectacular plays. The biggest source of gained or lost points,
deal after deal, is the auction.

## Bidding

### Count before you bid

A reliable hand evaluation:

| Resource | Value |
|---|---|
| Each Ace | ~25 points expected |
| Each Ten guarded by an Ace | ~15 points |
| A complete marriage | full marriage value (40-100) |
| A trump suit of 5+ cards | ~20-30 control points |

Add them up. Bid **only what you can show** without leaning on the talon.

### Don't bid for the talon

The talon is a **bonus**, not a plan. New players routinely bid 100 hoping
the talon will deliver a missing King — and lose 100 points half the time
when it doesn't.

### Push opponents off the barrel

Once an opponent reaches 880, every deal is binary: they either win or fall
back to 760. **Outbid them aggressively** during their barrel deals. Even
losing 100 to keep them off the win is a profitable trade.

## Card play

### Lead from your strongest suit

After declaring a marriage, **drive trumps out** by leading them. Two
defenders splitting four trumps cannot stop a 5-card trump suit.

### Hold the Ace until the Ten falls

Aces are most valuable when they capture a Ten (a 10-point card). Lead low
cards to flush the Tens, then take them with Aces.

### Think before you discard

The "must beat" rule means defenders are often **forced to play their
highest card**. Track these forced plays — by the third trick, an
attentive declarer often knows the entire defender hand.

## Defending

### Save your trumps

A defender's trumps are most valuable when they steal the **lead** from the
declarer. Keep one trump back to over-trump a critical Ace or Ten.

### Don't help the declarer

Defenders cannot signal to each other, but they can each refuse to give
the declarer a tempo. Don't burn tens on small declarer tricks; save them
for tricks the declarer wants.

### Capture marriages when you can

If the declarer holds a half-marriage (one of K or Q), a defender who
captures the **other half** in a trick prevents that marriage from ever
being declared.

## A 3-deal heuristic for opening bids

| Hand contains | Recommended opening |
|---|---|
| No marriage, no Ace, no long suit | **pass** |
| One marriage **or** 2 Aces | **100** |
| One high marriage (♥ or ♦) **and** an Ace | **110-120** |
| Two marriages | **120** |
| Two marriages including ♥ | **130-140**, raise after talon |

Adjust for table tendencies and your own running score: when you're behind,
bid more aggressively; when you're ahead, defend.
