# Bidding

The auction decides who becomes **declarer** for the deal. Each player, in
turn, either **raises the bid** or **passes**. The last remaining player in
the auction wins it.

## Order of bidding

Bidding starts with **forehand** (left of the dealer) and proceeds clockwise.

## Opening bid

The first bidder must bid **at least 100** or pass. (Some tables allow
forehand to "force" the auction by opening at 100 even with no intention to
play — see [House Rules](../variations/house-rules).)

## Bid increments

Each new bid must be **higher than the previous bid**, in steps of:

- **5 points** if the current bid is below 200, *or*
- **10 points** at any time.

In practice almost everyone bids in **steps of 10**.

## Passing

A player who passes is **out of the auction for the rest of the deal**. They
cannot re-enter.

## When the auction ends

The auction ends as soon as **two players have passed**. The last remaining
bidder is the **declarer** at that bid.

## Maximum opening bid

Without seeing the talon, no player may bid above **120** in the first round
of the auction. A higher bid implicitly relies on cards a player cannot yet
see and is reserved for the [Raise](./talon.md#step-4-raising-the-bid) phase after the
declarer has seen the talon.

:::tip[How high should you open?]
A typical opening at 100 promises:
- One reliable trump suit (4+ cards), **or**
- A marriage (King + Queen of any suit).

A first-round bid of 120 promises strong cards: a long trump suit or a high
marriage (♥ or ♦). See [Strategy](../strategy) for more.
:::

## Worked example

| Player | Bid |
|---|---|
| Forehand | 100 |
| Middlehand | 110 |
| Rearhand | pass |
| Forehand | 120 |
| Middlehand | pass |

**Forehand wins the auction at 120** and becomes declarer.
