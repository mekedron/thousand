# Trick-taking

After the talon and any opening marriage, the deal becomes a standard
trick-taking game with **two important Thousand-specific rules**.

## The basic flow

- The **declarer leads** the first trick (in standard Russian rules).
- Each player in turn plays one card clockwise.
- The trick is won by:
  - the **highest trump** if any trump was played, otherwise
  - the **highest card of the led suit**.
- The winner of the trick **leads the next trick**.

## Rule 1 — You must follow suit

If you have a card of the led suit, you **must** play it. (No discarding
when you can follow.)

## Rule 2 — You must beat the trick if you can

This is the rule that catches new players. After following suit, you must
play a **higher card of that suit if you have one** that beats every card
already played to the trick. If you cannot beat it, you may play any card
of the suit (typically your lowest).

## Rule 3 — You must trump if you cannot follow

If you have **no card of the led suit** and trump exists, you **must play
trump**. And you must play a trump that beats any trump already on the
trick if you have one.

## Rule 4 — Discarding is the last resort

Only when you have **neither the led suit nor a trump** may you discard a
card from another suit — and that discard cannot win the trick.

## Summary table

| Situation | Required action |
|---|---|
| Have the led suit | Follow suit. Beat if you can. |
| No led suit, have trump | Play trump. Over-trump if you can. |
| Neither led suit nor trump | Discard freely; cannot win. |

:::warning[Strict overtrumping]
The "must beat if you can" rule is strict. **Failing to overtake a trick
when you could have** is a **revoke** — typically penalised by awarding the
declarer's full bid to the opponents (or the equivalent penalty in your
house rules).
:::

## Number of tricks

Each player has **8 cards** after the talon and pass, so each deal has
**8 tricks**.

## At the end of all tricks

Each side counts its captured **card points** ([see card values](../equipment/card-ranking)),
adds any **marriage bonuses** declared during the deal, and proceeds to
[Scoring](./scoring).

:::note[Zero tricks → a "stick"]
A seat that takes **no tricks at all** in a deal earns a **stick**
(*болт* / *палка* / "pole"). The canonical Russian template fires a
fixed −120 penalty on every third stick — see
[House Rules — Zero-tricks penalty](../variations/house-rules.md#zero-tricks-penalty-болт--палка).
:::
