# A Deal at a Glance

A single deal of Thousand follows a fixed sequence. Each step is described
in detail in its own page; this overview is here so you can keep your
bearings.

## The seven phases of a deal

```
1. Deal       → 7 cards each + 3 to the talon
2. Bid        → Auction starting at 100, in steps of 5 or 10
3. Talon      → Declarer reveals the 3 cards and takes them
4. Pass       → Declarer passes 1 card to each opponent
5. Raise      → Declarer may raise their bid based on the new hand
6. Tricks     → 8 tricks played, with marriages declared on the lead
7. Score      → Card points + marriage bonuses recorded; check for 1000
```

## The contract

The declarer's bid is a **contract**: they have committed to scoring at
least that many points in the deal (counting card points + marriage
bonuses). If they make the contract they score their bid; if they fail,
they lose the bid amount from their running total.

Defenders score the card points and marriages they capture in the same
deal, separately for each defender.

## End of the game

A deal is played, scored, and added to the running totals. The first player
to **reach 1000 points** wins. Reaching exactly 880 puts a player **on the
barrel** — a high-pressure state described in [Scoring](./scoring).
