# The Talon

The **talon** (also *widow*, *prikup*, *приколодок*) is the three face-down
cards set aside during the deal. It is the most dramatic moment of the deal
— the auction winner gambles on three unseen cards.

## Step 1 — Reveal

The declarer turns the **three talon cards face up so all players can see
them**. This is non-negotiable: the talon is public information from this
moment on.

## Step 2 — Take

The declarer takes the three talon cards into their hand. They now have
**10 cards** while each opponent still has **7**.

## Step 3 — Pass

The declarer must pass **one card from their hand (face down) to each
opponent**. After passing:

- Declarer: 8 cards
- Each defender: 8 cards
- Total: 24 cards

The cards passed are at the declarer's discretion — usually the lowest in
suits the declarer does **not** want to use as trump.

:::caution The pass is binding
Once the declarer has placed cards face-down in front of the opponents and
released them, those cards are committed. No look-backs or trades.
:::

## Step 4: Raising the bid

After passing, the declarer may **raise their bid** to any higher value
that is achievable with their improved hand. The increment rules from
[Bidding](./bidding) still apply (steps of 5 below 200, then 10).

A common reason to raise:

- The talon contained the missing King or Queen completing a marriage.
- The talon contained a long suit that supports a high trump.

A declarer who is **disappointed** by the talon may **not lower** their bid.
They must play to make their original contract or pay the penalty.

:::tip Raise carefully
The raise locks in your contract — you score it on success, but lose the
**raised** amount on failure. Most players raise only when the talon turns
a 100 bid into a clear 120-150 hand.
:::

## Optional rule: pass-the-talon

In some house rules a declarer who finds the talon catastrophic may
**concede the deal immediately**, paying their bid and ending the hand.
This is **not** part of the standard rules and must be agreed in advance.

After the raise, play moves on to the [Trump & Marriages](./trump-and-marriages)
phase.
