# Polish *Tysiąc*

The Polish variant of Thousand. Played widely in Poland, often with a
**2-card talon** (called *musik* or *prikup*) split between the two
opponents.

## Differences from the standard rules

### Talon

- The talon contains **2 cards** (some tables: 3, distributed differently).
- After winning the auction, the declarer **passes one card face-down to
  each opponent** without first picking the talon up.
- In a 3-card talon variant the declarer takes all three, then passes one to
  each opponent (as in the Russian rules).

Net effect: the declarer plays from a **7-card hand** with no extra picks.

### Bidding

- Auction starts at **100**.
- Increments of **10** throughout (no 5-step phase).
- Maximum first-round bid: **120** without seeing the talon.

### Marriages (*meldunek*)

Identical to the Russian values — **100 / 80 / 60 / 40** for ♥ / ♦ / ♣ / ♠.
The Polish term *meldunek* is sometimes translated as "report" — declared
when the K or Q of the suit is led.

### Strict trump play

The "**must trump if void**" rule (*przebijanie*) is named and rigidly
enforced. Many Polish tables also require a defender to **overtake the
declarer** in trump if possible, which is stricter than some Russian
tables.

### Barrel

The Polish barrel rule is essentially identical: **880** mounting, three
deals to win, fall back to **760** on failure. Some Polish tables only
allow one deal on the barrel.

### Mizère / Misère

Less commonly used than in Russian play. When used, the bid is fixed at
**120** and declarer loses 120 if they take any trick.

## Notable house rules in Poland

- **"Ciemny"** (blind) opening at 100 — the player has not yet looked at
  their cards. A successful blind opening doubles its score.
- **Forced first bid** by forehand at 100 (matches the Russian convention).
- **Two-card talon** distributed face-down with no public reveal — keeps
  the talon completely private to the declarer.

## When to choose this variant

Choose Polish *Tysiąc* when:

- You want a **slightly more skill-driven** game (smaller talon, less
  randomness).
- You're playing with experienced trick-takers used to strict overtake
  rules.
- You enjoy **shorter bidding** (10-step increments end the auction faster).
