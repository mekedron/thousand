# Four-player Thousand

Played in **fixed partnerships across the table**. Two players sit
North-South, two East-West; they share running scores and cooperate openly
during play.

## Setup

- **24-card deck** as standard.
- Two players sit across from each other on each team.
- One of the four players is the **dealer**; in some variants the dealer
  sits out the deal; in others, the dealer plays as a fourth.

## Two configurations

### Configuration A — dealer plays, no talon

- Each of the four players is dealt **6 cards**.
- **No talon.** All 24 cards are in hands.
- Auction proceeds clockwise from the player to dealer's left.
- Trick play: 6 tricks total.

### Configuration B — dealer sits out (3 active + dealer)

- Same as standard 3-player Thousand, but with **4 people at the table**.
- The dealer **sits out** the hand; their partner plays alone for the
  partnership.
- The dealer rotates clockwise after each deal so each player sits out
  once per cycle of 4 hands.

Configuration A is more common in Ukrainian play; Configuration B is more
common in Russian and Polish four-player tables.

## Partnerships

- Partners are **across the table**; they keep a **shared running score**.
- During play partners **may not show cards or signal**, and may not
  speak about their hands.
- Only **one partner** is the declarer in any given deal; the other plays
  as a defender alongside the opposing pair.

Wait — that last point is the trick. In four-player Thousand, when one
partner becomes declarer:

- The **declarer plays for their team alone** (still a single-player
  contract, scored to the **partnership total**).
- The **declarer's partner** is **out of the deal** in Configuration A (in
  some tables they play normally as a defender; the rule must be agreed).
- In Configuration B the declarer's partner is the sitting-out dealer
  anyway.

## Marriages and scoring

- Marriage values unchanged — **100 / 80 / 60 / 40** for ♥ / ♦ / ♣ / ♠.
- Each partnership has a single running score; the **first partnership to
  1000 wins**.
- Barrel rules apply to the partnership total, not to individuals.

## When to choose this variant

Choose four-player Thousand when:

- You have exactly four players and want everyone in the game.
- You want a **partnership** dynamic (Bridge-flavoured trick play).
- You enjoy slower, more cooperative play than the cut-throat 3-player
  game.
