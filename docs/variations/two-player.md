# Two-player Thousand

A head-to-head variant. The two-player game is closer in feel to **Schnapsen**
or **Sixty-Six** — its closest relatives in the Marriage family — but keeps
the Thousand scoring conventions.

## Setup

- **24-card deck** as standard.
- Each player is dealt **9 cards** (or **7 cards**, depending on the variant
  — see below).
- The talon is **3 cards** dealt face-down, with the **top card flipped face
  up** to indicate trump candidacy.

## Two main two-player variants

### Variant A — closed talon (reference)

- Each player receives **9 cards**.
- A 6-card **stock** is placed face-down with the **bottom card turned face
  up** to indicate the trump suit (similar to Schnapsen).
- After each trick, both players **draw one card from the stock** until it
  is exhausted.
- After the stock is exhausted, the **must-follow / must-beat** rules
  become strict (they are relaxed during the drawing phase).
- Game to **1000 points**.

### Variant B — fixed deal, no draw

- Each player receives **7 cards**.
- A 3-card talon is set aside; declarer takes it after the (very short)
  auction and passes 1 card back face-down to the opponent.
- No drawing during play — 8 tricks total, identical to the 3-player
  pattern.
- Game to **1000 points**.

## Bidding

- Auction is **head-to-head**: opener bids; opponent **raises or passes**.
- Minimum **100**; no maximum without seeing the talon.
- The auction usually ends within two or three exchanges.

## Marriages

Identical values to the standard rules — **100 / 80 / 60 / 40** for
♥ / ♦ / ♣ / ♠.

In **Variant A** (with a draw stock), a marriage may be declared by **leading
either the King or the Queen** while the marriage is still in hand — the
score posts immediately. The trump suit changes accordingly.

## Card play differences

- **Variant A** — during the drawing phase, you are not strictly required
  to follow suit or beat. Once the stock is empty, the standard rules
  apply.
- **Variant B** — full standard rules from trick one.

## Scoring

Identical to the standard rules — bid scored on success, deducted on
failure; defender's captured points scored as their own. Marriages scored
to the player who declared them.

## When to choose this variant

Choose two-player Thousand when:

- You want a **fast, intense head-to-head** game.
- You're already familiar with **Schnapsen** or **Sixty-Six** and want a
  longer-format relative.
- You want to teach a learner without involving a third player as a
  potential punching bag.
