# Ukrainian *Тисяча*

The Ukrainian variant overlaps heavily with the Russian one but has a few
distinctive house conventions, especially around the **bolt** (forced bid)
rule and four-player play.

## Differences from the standard rules

### The "bolt" (бовт / болт)

In some Ukrainian tables, when forehand passes and the next two players
also pass without bidding, the **dealer is forced to take the contract at
100** — they cannot escape the auction. This prevents endless
pass-everything-around stalling.

:::note Two rules share the name "bolt"
The Russian word *болт* (and its synonym *палка*) is used in some
traditions for a **completely different rule**: a zero-tricks penalty
documented under
[House Rules → Zero-tricks penalty](./house-rules.md#zero-tricks-penalty-болт--палка).
The Ukrainian forced-dealer-bid sense and the Russian zero-tricks-penalty
sense are unrelated — confirm which one your table means before play.
:::

### Four-player play in pairs

A 4-player Ukrainian table is normally played in **fixed partnerships**
(across the table), with the dealer also playing. Two cards instead of
three go to the talon. See [Four-player Variant](./four-player) for the
full mechanics.

### Marriages (*мар'яж*)

Identical values — **100 / 80 / 60 / 40** for ♥ / ♦ / ♣ / ♠.

### Barrel mechanics

Often slightly stricter:

- **Two deals only** on the barrel in some Ukrainian tables (not three).
- **Falling off** drops to 760 (same as Russian).
- **Both players on the barrel**: in some tables, both fall off (both
  knocked back to 760); in others, only the later mounter survives.

### Penalty for "stealing" the talon look

A player who illicitly looks at the talon before the auction ends typically
pays a flat **120-point penalty** and the deal is redealt by the next
dealer. Stricter than the standard.

## Common Ukrainian extensions

- **"Шостерик" (six-card)** — a rare 6-card-hand variant played with all
  the pip cards through 8 included; specialised regional play.
- **Spades double down** — a few Ukrainian tables score the spades
  marriage at **60** (matching clubs), reasoning that the 40-point spade
  marriage is too weak to be worth declaring. Always confirm at the table.

## When to choose this variant

Choose Ukrainian Тисяча when:

- You have **4 players** and want partnership play.
- You want a **strict, fast** auction (the bolt rule prevents stalling).
- You're playing with friends who want **shorter, more punishing** barrels.
